About

After the independence in 1947, our countrymen who had been to Burma to earn their livelihood were sent back to India and put to live in the Metro Cities. A certain lot among those section came to Delhi in search of their lively hood. Not only these settlers, but also people from other parts of Tamil Nadu have also come to the capital for work and settled down in all over Delhi. Most of these people belong to poor working class. The local language Hindi is not so familiar to these people nor they are very conversant with English. To cater to the spiritual need of these people in their won mother tongue Tamil, (Late) Most Rev. Angelo Fernandes, the Archbishop of Delhi, by his magnanimous gesture, invited (Late) Rev.Fr.Lazer Montford from Pondicherry Diocese in 1968 and the Tamil Ministry started functioning. From then onwards, a special Chaplain either from Tamil Nadu or from Delhi Diocese is appointed to promote the spiritual ministry of the migrating Tamil Catholics.

Followed by Rev.Fr.Lazer Montfort, Tamil Catholic Miistry was steered by the dedicated services of Rev.Fr.Susai Sebastian, Rev.Fr.Jesudas, Rev.Fr.Beski D’Almeda, Rev.Fr.M.Amaladas, Rev.Fr.Maria Susai, Rev.Fr,Jeyaseelan, Rev.Fr.Michaelraj, Rev.Fr.Julius Caesar, Rev.Fr.Arockiadass, Rev.Fr.S.Jaya Seelan, Rev. Fr.Jardis Nazareth during these decades and at present the Tamil Catholic Ministry is steered by the present Chaplain Rev.Fr.John Rosario.
